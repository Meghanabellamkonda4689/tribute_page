Mother Teresa

1910 - 1997

" A Beacon of Love, Compassion, and Selflessness, Inspiring Acts of Kindness Worldwide."


TimeLine

Birth

1910 - 1911

Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu was born on August 26 in Skopje, Macedonia, which was then part of the Ottoman Empire. She later became known as Mother Teresa.

Joining the Sisters of Loreto

1928 -1929

At 18, she left home to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland, where she received religious training and took the name Sister Mary Teresa.

Leaving Loreto

1948 - 1949

Mother Teresa experienced a "call within a call" from God, inspiring her to leave the Sisters of Loreto and begin the Missionaries of Charity in Calcutta, India, to help the destitute and dying.

Foundation of the Missionaries of Charity

1950 - 1951

On October 7, Mother Teresa officially founded the Missionaries of Charity, dedicated to providing care and love to the most impoverished and neglected people in society.

Nobel Peace Prize

1979 - 1980

Mother Teresa was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her unwavering dedication to humanitarian work, drawing global attention to her mission's efforts to alleviate suffering.

Death

1997 - 1998

Mother Teresa passed away on September 5 in Calcutta, leaving behind a legacy of compassion, selflessness, and service, and she continues to inspire countless individuals to this day.

Canonization

2016 - 2017

Mother Teresa was canonized, becoming Saint Teresa of Calcutta, by the Roman Catholic Church, recognizing her holiness and lifelong dedication to serving the poor and sick.

Biography

Mother Teresa, born in Skopje in 1910, dedicated her life to helping the destitute. As a Catholic nun, she founded the Missionaries of Charity in Calcutta, India, where she cared for the poorest of the poor. Her selflessness and compassion earned her international acclaim and the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. Mother Teresa's legacy of love and kindness transcends borders, inspiring countless individuals to serve humanity. She passed away in 1997, but her impact lives on. In 2016, the Catholic Church canonized her as a saint, recognizing her profound humanitarian work. Mother Teresa's life serves as a testament to the enduring power of selfless service and unwavering love for the marginalized.
